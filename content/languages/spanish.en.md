## Overview

Spanish (español or castellano) is a Romance language that originated in the Iberian Peninsula. Today, it is spoken across the globe, with significant populations in Spain, Latin America, and the United States.

## Geographic Distribution

### Spain
The original home of Spanish, with distinct regional dialects including Castilian, Catalan, Galician, and Basque influences.

### Latin America
Spanish dominates across Central and South America:
- **Mexico**: Largest Spanish-speaking country by population
- **Central America**: Guatemala, Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Costa Rica, Panama
- **South America**: Colombia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina, Paraguay, Uruguay
- **Caribbean**: Cuba, Dominican Republic, Puerto Rico

### Other Regions
- United States: Over 40 million Spanish speakers
- Equatorial Guinea (Africa)
- Philippines (historical influence)

## Linguistic Characteristics

### Grammar
Spanish is a gendered language with masculine and feminine nouns. Verbs conjugate extensively based on tense, mood, and subject. Word order is typically Subject-Verb-Object but is flexible.

### Pronunciation
Spanish pronunciation is relatively consistent, with clear vowel sounds and mostly phonetic spelling. The "th" sound (theta) in Castilian Spanish is notable for speakers of other languages.

### Vocabulary
Spanish has borrowed words from Arabic (due to historical occupation), English (modern technology), and indigenous languages of the Americas.

## Importance for Global Understanding

With over 500 million speakers, Spanish is essential for:
- Global business and diplomacy
- Understanding Latin American culture and history
- Accessing literature, film, and arts
- Connecting with diverse communities worldwide
